Social Media and its Effect on Political Polarization and Participation

What is Political Polarization?

Political polarization is when people become divided along opposing political views, and only communicate with those that share their same viewpoint or perspective. Other phrases for it are echo chambers, filter bubbles, and homophily clusters.

Role Algorithms play in selective information

Today, social media is highly personalized and catered to one's interests. This sounds good, but creates an echo chamber because our feeds only show what we want to see. This is called Network Homophily, where nodes of a network cluster near other similar nodes algorithmically.

Why this is an issue (in relation to the 2016 election)

In accordance with network homophily on Facebook, users were unaware how much of their peers really agreed or disagreed with them. Since algorithms on social media suppress opposing views/divisive, it is hard for us to gauge who really agreed or disagreed with us. Furthermore, this leads to very little perspective politically. When all your peers reaffirm your beliefs, it makes you believe you are right.

Political Participation

The US trails behind other major countries in voter turnout due to voter apathy, but social media has the ability to mobilize potential voters. One study found that online outreach was more likely to get younger voters to be involved in the election.

Potential Solutions

Hold Tech Companies Accountable

Tech moguls like Mark Zuckerberg have denied Facebook's culpability in the creation of echo chambers, but the results are clear. Tech companies are partly responsible as they created the personalizatoin that comes with social media under the guise of "better" connection, when in reality, it stifles our understanding of the world.

Improve Algorithms

Just like how algorithms have created the dilemma we are in, they can just as easily reverse it. With apps that allow us to meet people of opposing viewpoints, we can just as easily have a good social media experience while not being close minded.

Ethics

For software developers, learn about the ethics behind what you are doing. Realize the consequences behind the products you create and how they might contribute to one that becomes like "Black Mirror."

Digital Literacy

Don't just block those that disagree with you. Learn to understand where they are coming from, but at the same time, don't shy away from tough conversations or discourse.
